The curious few

 

Let’s take a moment to appreciate the curious. The wonderers.

Those who’d prefer to peer through a hole in a fence than block it up.

Those who not only ask the 'why?' but want to know the 'who?' 'what?' 'when?' 'where?' and 'how?' too.

Without them, nothing new would ever happen and no new trends would ever start.

 

Nothing ventured and, certainly, nothing gained.

Because good enough would be perfect. There’d be no need for great.

 

Inspiring stories are all around us, just waiting to be discovered.

Yet it’s only those with a curious mind that will uncover them.

New weekday compact size

The Age weekday editions have now launched in a new, quality compact format. The change is in response to your demands for a more reader-friendly format and we're excited by the chance to create a new premium showcase in Australia for quality, independent journalism.

The new format is the same Age that you know and love, and our journalism is as strong as ever. Along with the more convenient compact size, the new weekday edition of The Age includes two new liftouts: Pulse on Mondays - the last word on health, fitness and wellbeing, and The Shortlist on Fridays - with weekend-ready news and reviews of the latest movies and stage shows as well as all your favourite music updates from EG.

If you want to know more, please see our FAQs below.


Our new digital subscriptions are here

Our new digital subscription model for theage.com.au has something for everyone and even more for subscribers.

  • Unlimited articles on theage.com.au
  • $1 for your first month - special introductory offer
  • Cancel anytime - no fees

All readers receive a number of free articles every month

Our subscription model lets everyone read 10 articles free on our website every month. So you are still able to visit theage.com.au exactly as you do now and read any 10 articles you choose.

This begins for readers in North America, Europe and the Middle East first as part of our gradual roll-out strategy. We will launch to Australia, New Zealand and other Asia-Pacific regions later in 2013.

Unlimited articles for subscribers

Readers who read more than the 10 free articles a month can gain unlimited access to our digital content by subscribing.

We have one subscription package to begin with: $15 a month for unlimited website access from any device, with a special $1 for your first month introductory offer available. This package gives users access to unlimited articles at theage.com.au on both the desktop and mobile sites. To subscribe, click here.

Later this year we will introduce additional subscription packages that include The Age tablet app and print delivery. If you'd like more details, refer to our FAQs below.

Subscription benefits and new features

As well as getting unlimited access to our news and features, digital subscribers get access to:

  • Zoom: a new tool we’ve developed in partnership with the University of Sydney that’s a powerful way to search our archives.
  • Shortbooks: a new series of interactive eBooks curated by our journalists on a range of specialist topics.
  • Unique offers and event invitations through the Australian My Benefits rewards program.

For more details on our digital subscriptions refer to our FAQs below.

Why are you doing this?

The media industry has changed rapidly over the past few years. That change has enabled us to do incredible things with news and journalism that we’ve never been able to do before. We want to keep evolving so we can continue to bring you independent journalism with the technological innovation and style you love. So if you’re a regular reader, we hope you’ll consider subscribing.

How much does a digital subscription cost?

We have a special introductory offer for unlimited website access from any device - $1 for your first month. Every month that follows thereafter will cost just $15. This package gives users access to unlimited articles at theage.com.au on both the desktop and mobile sites.

Later this year we’ll introduce additional subscription packages that include The Age tablet app and print delivery.

What does a digital subscription include?

We have introduced one digital subscription package to selected overseas regions first as part of our gradual roll-out strategy – North America, Europe and the Middle East. This package provides access to unlimited articles on theage.com.au on both the desktop and mobile versions of the sites. To subscribe, click here.

When we launch in Australia and New Zealand later this year we will introduce subscription packages that include access to our tablet apps, digital replica editions and newspaper home delivery.

All packages also include new website features as well as access to our Australian My Benefits subscriber rewards program.

One of these new features is Zoom, a new tool we’ve developed in partnership with the University of Sydney that’s a powerful way to search our archives. Another is shortbooks, a new series of interactive eBooks curated by our journalists on a range of specialist topics. Both shortbooks and Zoom are available for all readers when first launched, but will be exclusive to subscribers when we introduce digital subscriptions to Australia and New Zealand later this year.

Tell me more: Adam Mather, GM Digital Innovations, blogs here about Zoom and our new shortbooks.

How many articles can I read for free?

All readers in North America, Europe and the Middle East can read 10 articles for free every month. We invite readers who wish to read more to subscribe.

The number of free articles allocated to Australian readers will be different to what we have provided international readers, reflecting the readership patterns of our local audience.

How does the article limit work?

On the website, you can read 10 articles a month for free. The 10 free articles reset every month, exactly one month on from your first article read after digital subscriptions were introduced.

Our technology counts the number of articles you read a month using web browser cookies. Browser cookies are a small piece of data a website sends your web browser when you visit their site. You don’t have to sign in or create an account with us to read your free articles. You’ll be able to read your free articles just by visiting theage.com.au as you do now – no further steps are required.

I'm in Australia, why am I being asked to subscribe already?

A small number of readers in Australia may have IP addresses categorised as being from North America, Europe or the Middle East. These readers will be asked to subscribe if they read more than 10 articles in a month. If you are in Australia and are affected, you can call us at 13 66 66 (Australia only). If you are overseas and would like to get in contact, you can email us here.

What devices are supported?

The digital subscription packages provide unlimited access to theage.com.au on desktops, laptops, tablet devices and smartphones.

Are Android devices supported?

Yes. Our digital subscription packages include unlimited access to theage.com.au on Android tablets and smartphones.

And when we release the digital subscriptions packages that include our tablet app, those packages will include access to The Age tablet app on the Android platform too.

What if I already get the newspaper home delivered to an Australian address?

If you get The Age home delivered at least two days a week, your current print subscription will include full digital access to our websites and tablet apps (when introduced later this year) at no extra cost. We’ll provide you with instructions on how to activate that benefit before we introduce digital subscriptions in Australia and New Zealand later this year. Make sure we’ve got your email address to ensure we can get in touch with you when that time comes. You can register your email address against your print subscription here.

I want to upgrade to two or more days a week to ensure I receive full digital access

No problem, simply log on to theage.com.au/manage and click on Upgrade.

I'm an Australian print subscriber travelling overseas

If you’re travelling overseas for an extended period to the selected overseas launch regions – North America, Europe and the Middle East – you’ll be able to activate your digital access while you travel.

When you’ve read more than 10 free articles on our websites you’ll be served with a prompt to subscribe. Simply follow the steps listed there to activate your free digital access and enter your subscriber number to keep reading.

Why do I only receive 10 free articles?

We have set the article limit at 10 articles as it allows readers to sample our journalism before subscribing. If you are a regular reader, however, we hope you consider subscribing.

The number of free articles allocated to Australian readers will be different to what we have provided international readers, reflecting the readership patterns of our local audience.

How are digital subscriptions billed?

Digital subscriptions are direct-debited monthly from your nominated credit card. Your billing date each month will be the same date you started your subscription with us. For example if you start your subscription on March 27, you will be billed on March 27, April 27, May 27, and so forth.

You can cancel your digital subscription at any time. No cancellation fees will apply.

What are the payment options?

You can charge your subscription to any Visa, MasterCard and American Express card.

Can I still read articles if I do not subscribe?

You can enjoy 10 free articles each month on theage.com.au. You can also visit the homepage as often as you wish and view as many photo galleries and videos as you like without those articles counting in your free article allocation.

If you click on an article link from social media, or within an email, or from a search engine, that article will also not count towards your free article allocation.

What about the tablet app?

The tablet apps for The Age remain free in every country until we introduce digital subscriptions in Australia and New Zealand later this year. When that time comes, the tablet apps will follow a "freemium" subscription model, which has several sections remaining free, and others being available only for paying subscribers. We’ll provide the details about tablet app subscriptions later this year as we move closer to their launch. In the meantime, we hope you’ll continue to enjoy the tablet apps in their current form.

What if I have a subscription to the Digital Edition?

If you live in North America, Europe or the Middle East and you already subscribe to the Digital Edition, the replica of the print format, you will need to purchase a website access subscription if you wish to read more than 10 articles a month on our websites. A website access subscription costs $15 a month and you can subscribe here.

Later this year, we will have bundled digital packages that include access to the Digital Edition as well as website access. So if you subscribe to the Digital Edition today, we’ll be in touch later this year with details about how you can upgrade to that new bundle if it suits you.

My Benefits also gives you access to the best of what's happening in and around our city, thanks to our extensive network of partners. We're securing exclusive pre-sales, ticket offers and additional experiences that will only be available to our subscribers and we’re adding more each day.

The Age in full, to fit your pocket

It’s hard to imagine 2013 without mobile technology. The number of smartphones bought in a year has just overtaken the number of desktop computers and in just a few years we’ve gone from something functional that took calls to something magical that does it all.

We know how important it is for you to know what’s going on whenever and wherever, so we have put our focus into producing a fully-fledged site optimised for your smartphone that works on all major devices and operating systems. It’s every centimetre The Age, just millimetres of the size.

There are more sections and sub-categories, an abundance of video and photo galleries, commenting and clever sharing with your friends, curated commentary but above all, continuous updates at your fingertips.

See for yourself by visiting m.theage.com.au on your phone and login to join in.

Just save the icon to your homescreen and it’s ready to go whenever you are.
