                                              Employment law

                                              Arntzen de Besche is among Norway's leading firms in the areas of employment law and pensions. Our employment law department is one of the country's largest and has particular knowledge and experience in collective and individual employment law in national and international contexts.

                                              Our lawyers have experience from employer and employee organisations, major private businesses, government administration and the courts.

                                              Clear advice
                                              Knowledge of our clients' businesses is very important to us. This enables us to give clear advice and best protect our clients' interests.

                                              As a partner for the long haul we offer general advice on issues of employment law and provide assistance on specific disputes before the Labour Court of Norway, the ordinary courts of law nationally and internationally, and in arbitration cases. We also offer assistance relating to pension law.

                                              Our clients and services
                                              Among our clients we find businesses, organisations, public institutions and private individuals.

                                              Our services include:

                                              • drawing up and interpreting contracts of employment, consultancy agreements and service delivery contracts
                                              • establishment of incentive schemes, including bonus and share option programmes
                                              • drawing up and enforcing non-competition clauses
                                              • legal advice on the rules for transfers of undertakings
                                              • legal advice in connection with restructuring, such as downsizing, insourcing and outsourcing, acquisitions, mergers and due diligence
                                              • assessments regarding the treatment of personal information, including monitoring e-mail
                                              • issues relating to harassment, warnings and discrimination
                                              • drawing up ethical guidelines and assistance on issues related to corruptionestablishment, interpretation, amending and termination of collective agreements

                                              In addition, we have considerable experience and expertise in providing legal advice and assistance in negotiations and litigation in connection with termination of and changes in employment relationships, particularly at management level.

                                              Offices in organisations, literature and speaking engagements
                                              Several of our lawyers have regular speaking engagements and have written books and articles on employment law. One of our lawyers also headed the committee whose work led to the current Working Environment Act, while another lawyer is the chairman and the Norwegian Bar Association's law committee on labour law.
