Type of Infant Travel: Infant Travel (?)
  • Infants who are under 2 years of age at the time of travel may travel as a "lap child" without purchasing a separate seat. Only 1 infant may travel as a lap child per ticketed adult, additional children must have a separate seat. Proof of age may be requested by the airline.
  • Guests who select the "Seat" option will be purchasing a separate seat for the infant and will be able to use the infant's own child-restraint seating; which is recommended by the FAA and The American Academy of Pediatrics.
  • While lap children generally fly free to domestic destinations, a lap child will require an infant fare ticket for international destinations which must be purchased directly with the airline. The cost of the lap child ticket will vary depending on your destination. BookIt.com recommends contacting the airline directly after booking to secure a lap child on your itinerary.
  • Only 1 child may travel as a "lap-child" with each ticketed adult.

      Type of Infant Travel: (?) (?)
      • Infants who are under 2 years of age at the time of travel may travel as a "lap child" without purchasing a separate seat. Only 1 infant may travel as a lap child per ticketed adult, additional children must have a separate seat. Proof of age may be requested by the airline.
