From Our Research Team:

A Smart, Digital Powered Speaker

The Line 6 StageSource L3t powered speaker sets a new standard for high-performance, multi-functional loudspeakers. Rich, detailed sound is delivered by the 3-way, tri-amped design providing 1,400 watts of power. Whether you're performing solo or playing to crowded halls, true scalability means the StageSource L3t excels in any situation, and you can add StageSource L3s subwoofers to scale your system to any room. Factor in the integrated multi-channel mixer (with EQ, feedback suppression, and more), Smart Speaker modes with DSP sound optimization, and L6 Link digital networking support (for use with the Line 6 StageScape M20d digital mixer), and you'll see that StageSource L3t loudspeakers have set the bar high for performance, functionality, and innovation.







Line 6 StageSource L3t Powered Speaker at a Glance:
  • Built-in Mixer
  • Maximum Versatility
  • L6 Link Digital Networking
  • Smart Speaker Modes

Built-in Mixer
The integrated multi-channel mixer packs EQ, acoustic guitar modeling, feedback suppression, and more for unprecedented single-speaker performance opportunities. The Line 6 StageSource L3T is a powerful all-in-one solution for your PA needs. Each input channel includes a 3-band EQ with sweepable mids, Smart Mod, and Smart Reverb effects to optimize your sound. An output limiter and 12-band feedback suppression safeguard you against deafening squeals and distortion at your gig.

Maximum Versatility
The scalable nature of the StageSource L3t makes it equally suited for use as a floor monitor, front of house speaker, backline amp, or even as a standalone PA system. The multi-function design allows you to get the best performance in any application. An onboard accelerometer and pole-mount sensors sense the speaker's orientation and set the speaker mode automatically. Anyone can easily use the StageSource L3t speakers and sound great.

L6 Link Digital Networking
Line 6's L6 Link technology allows you to create a network of speakers that can configure itself automatically. Linked speakers then communicate and set up stereo signals, adjust the Smart Speaker modes, and perform system-wide optimization. Connect your network to a Line 6 StageScape M20d digital mixer to experience a new paradigm in live sound mixing. This combination creates a seamless integration of all your Line 6 Stage components into a potent audio solution.

Smart Speaker Modes
Six DSP-based speaker modes are ready to optimize the sound of the StageSource L3t speaker to match any performance material. Each mode features optimized EQs for various scenarios including front-of-house PA, floor monitor, personal PA, acoustic guitar, electric guitar, and keyboard backline. Just push the appropriate switch and the Line 6 StageSource L3t is ready and tweaked for your needs.

  • Extremely versatile as PA speaker, floor monitor, or instrument speaker

Awesome -

by Kevin James from Worcester, MA
 May 16, 2013
 Music Background: Musician in a band
I bought this primarily for use as an acoustic amp and for when our band does small shows. Bass player plays upright and he's going to get one too. I'm hoping we can tie the vocals between the 2 of th... read more [+]em if and when he gets one. So far last night we had a rehearsal with it. Prior to that I was going from Fishman Aura Pedal (the big one) into a K12 which sounded pretty **** good. But this thing is the cats ***. My resonators have highlander pickups and I go from those into the Aura, into the mixer portion of this speaker. I found the setting (pa) not (acoustic) guitar to work best for what I'm using. I suppose that plugging my J45 with matrix UST pickup would have sounded good with the acoustic setting but with the aura pedal, set as PA mode seemed to sound best. All Eq were set default or flat and the thing sounded amazing. Even with our "hit em hard as you can drummer" we could hear this thing no problem, even with the volume set at 12:00. I realize I'm not using it for it's primary purpose, but this is a unique speaker that can do it all, and it's about time someone came up with something good like this. It's sort of like a Fishman soloamp but on steroids. At 1400 watts, this is what intrigued me about using this as an acoustic amp. Now I can use my Mesa MKV for the heavy stuff and this for the acoustic stuff. Loud as **** and sounds stellar. All across the tonal spectrum. Nice warm bass, no harsh top end and nice scooped mids even with default settings. I like it so much I might get another as well. What I'd love to see is some variation on the reverb. There's only one setting and I like my reverb a little more dense. Seems to sound thin for vocals, but that's not the primary reason I bought it. What they call modulation effect is just chorus, sounds decent. The price is right too, for what you get, but the bag is a tad expensive. I could see like $80 for the bag, but > $100 is a bit too much. But I hate having stuff unprotected, so I got the bag as well. close [-]
