Welcome to Power Portfolio

This is Money's new portfolio is bigger, brighter and simpler - the best way for novices and experienced investors alike to keep track of your shareholdings.

We hope you like it.

We're constantly making improvements and are working through a long list of extra features - so if some of the things you expect to see aren't there yet, please bear with us.


A FEW THINGS YOU SHOULD KNOW ABOUT THE PORTFOLIO

  1. If you haven't logged in since the relaunch, your shares and funds were migrated automatically so there will be no need to rebuild entire portfolios.
  2. However, any entries with 0 holdings will not appear in your new portfolios. Simply enter them again if you wish to. The wishlist function has been removed.
  3. Companies that were deleted from your old portfolio are likely to reappear in the new one. Please delete them again. Apologies for that but the data we received from our previous supplier didn't identify these as having been deleted.
  4. Commission and stamp duty charges are now deducted from profit/loss figure to give a better reflection of the real world. It will mean, if you have used this facility, that the profit/loss figures will be slightly different from your old portfolio.
  5. Based on extensive feedback from This is Money readers and portfolio users, we've tried to simplify the Power Portfolio as much as possible. However you may find that adding items is tricky to start with. It's not...

...how to add securities to your Power Portfolio 

  • To add items, first select the type of investment from the drop down menu.
    - Cash equity for shares
    - Oeic or unit trust
    - And so on.

  • This activates the second box.
  • Now start typing the name of the company or fund. 
  • Select from the results
  • You must add number of shares
  • You can add fees and commission (optional)
  • And you're away.
