      What's great about it: Thin
      What's not so great: A little expensive, screen resolution
      Windows 8 I feel was primarily designed for a touch screen device. This laptop has touch screen, but its a little awkward as it is not a tablet, so the screen will wobble and be a little springy. Ended up using a mouse and the touch pad for most tasks, but still use the touch screen for the games. I still prefer Windows 7.
      The laptop is thin and very nice. The build quality is excellent. It is a bit heavy for its size, but it has a good solid feel to it.
      As for performance, this laptop is decent as is. I ended up putting a Samsung 256gb 830 SSD and upgrading the ram to 8gb. The laptop boots to the login screen in 3 seconds, pretty sweet. Everything loads almost instantly. As for the CPU, being an ultrabook, it will handle everyday tasks, but it is not really made for encoding video's or rendering.
      The price is right in the middle between a high end ultrabook and a budget laptop. You can definitely get a laptop with better specs for cheaper, but it will be bulky. This laptop is more of a low end ultrabook. You pay a premium for the thin size and portabilty. Touch screen is a bonus.
      Battery life is pretty good. Will get about 5 hours of use (surfing web, watching youtube, etc) before I need to plug it in.
      Wish the screen resolution was better.
