      What's great about it: Good price, performance looks good on paper, I LOVE the light up keyboard
      What's not so great: Speakers, Speakers, Speakers, oh at touchpad.
      I just bought this computer yesterday (5/4/13) and I will be returning it tomorrow (5/6/13) - before I do so - I thought I would try and warn anyone looking to get this computer.
      First off - to start with the positive, from what I can tell the performance side (i7 processor 8GB memory, 750GB hard drive) are all right for the price. The keyboard in my opinion is SO cool. At night it looks like NYC the way it lights up! The black base with silver keys and they way the feel as I type are all A+ rating in my book.
      But... here comes the bad side. First and foremost I am most upset with the sales people at Best Buy (in Bloomington, MN). I went in twice to look for computers. Both times I told the sales rep I was looking for a new laptop, the main thing I use it for is gaming - and I've always been happy with Toshiba and was looking for a good gaming Toshiba. Both sales rep said the same thing: "Oh if you're looking for a good gaming computer - you should go with this Asus over Toshiba. You get the same specs but Asus has better all around performance". Long story short - after I heard the same pitch from sales guy #2 I was convinced to go with Asus. Wow... what a mistake!!! This computer DOESN'T HAVE SPEAKERS!!! I thought something was wrong when I turned my audio up to max and still couldn't hear anything. After reading other reviews online I came to realize this is just the way this computer is build.
      If you are looking to use this computer for ANYTHING audio: movies, music, videos, games - and you don't want to have an additional set of speakers you need to carry around DON'T BUY THIS COMPUTER.
      Now, if audio isn't a concern to you - there is 1 other problem that I just couldn't handle. The touchpad. I don't know what they were going for here (well I do, obviously they were trying to make it like an I-pad). And it's just ridiculous. Whenever I'm trying to do ANYTHING I seem to set off either 1. Maximizing or minimizing the screen. 2. Switch over to the app page. 3. Pull up the side bar. 4. Doing anything other than what I wanted it to do. Now I don't know if this is an Asus problem, a Window's 8 problem, or some combination of both. Point is - after 24 hours - I'm done with this.
      At the end of the day, the main reason I'm returning this computer is due to the speakers. I could probably get used to the touchpad (after all it's just a learning curve). But for a GAMING computer I think it's a complete joke 2 different people at Best Buy would suggest this computer without telling me it's a MUST to buy a speaker system as well.
      Oh, and I have OCD - the fingerprints on the cover have already drove me insane.

      What's great about it: Good price, compact for such a powerful computer
      What's not so great: Windows 8 bugs cause it to randomly shutdown/die with no warning
      I bought this laptop in January 2013 when my old laptop under warranty was junked out. I wasn't excited about getting Windows 8, but it was the only option I had. I first bought a desktop, but I could never get it to work with my Internet. I tried two different desktops, I had GeekSquad try to help various times, and it nothing ever worked. I was eventually told by Microsoft, "It's a known issue that Windows 8 in desktops isn't compatible with certain wifi adapters. We suggest getting a different wifi carrier." Oh hell no, I'm not switching Internet providers for my computer. I'll just get another laptop. $700 for 8G RAM and a 750G HDD is hard to beat, and I've always heard good things about Asus, so why not?
      Well, as expected, Windows 8 was annoying to get used to. I fiddled with the settings to disable features I disliked, making Windows 8 more like Windows Vista, except for the annoying windows key/app screen feature which can't be disabled. Disabling the mouse pad shortcuts is a MUST; those shortcuts are extremely annoying and caused a lot of problems the first few weeks I had the laptop. Once I got the OS to a manageable state, everything seemed fine.
      Then, of course, I started experiencing the problem almost every user will have; my laptop started spontaneously shutting down like I'd taken out the battery when the laptop was unplugged. No warning, just a black screen. After the third or fourth time it happened, I did some poking around online; of course, it's a Windows 8 issue, this time having to do with dynamic ticks (idle processing). A windows blog has posted a temporary fix, but I'd rather not apply it because it will make my laptop very power inefficient. However, if it interests you, check out this link: http://withinwindows.com/2012/06/28/workaround-for-windows-8-freezing-issues/
      Anyway, I'm leaving a 4-star review for now. I'll update it to 5-stars if/when Windows 8 fixes the dynamic ticks issue.

      2a. Alternatively, type: netsh wlan show networks mode=bssid
